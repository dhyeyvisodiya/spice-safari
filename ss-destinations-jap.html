Explore Popular Destinations of Japan

Japan, officially known as the "Land of the Rising Sun," is an island nation located in East Asia. Japan is a captivating blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge innovation, making it a fascinating destination for travelers and a dynamic country on the global stage.

Tokyo

Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital, mixes the ultramodern and the traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples.

Explore More

Kyoto

Kyoto is famous for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses.

Explore More

Osaka

Osaka is known for its modern architecture, nightlife, and hearty street food. The 16th-century shogunate Osaka Castle, which has undergone several restorations, is its main historical landmark.

Explore More

Hiroshima

Hiroshima, a modern city on Japan's Honshu Island, is known for its peace memorial.

Explore More

Nara

Nara, Japan's first permanent capital, is known for its 8th-century temples and vast park.

Explore More

Sapporo

Sapporo, the largest city on Hokkaido, is famous for its beer, skiing, and annual Sapporo Snow Festival featuring enormous ice sculptures.

Explore More

Nikko

Nikko is famous for Toshogu, Japan's most lavishly decorated shrine and the mausoleum of Tokugawa Ieyasu, the founder of the Tokugawa shogunate.

Explore More

Kanazawa

Kanazawa, capital of Ishikawa Prefecture, sits on the Sea of Japan. It's known for well-preserved Edo-era districts, art museums, and regional handicrafts.
